Job Roles

Describe the job role and common tasks undertaken for each of the following positions in the games industry.

Designer - A designers job is to create goals, rules and systems to make a game what it is

Artist - An artist is responsible for all parts in a game that need areas of visual arts in them

Programmer - A programmer writes code so the computer knows how to make the game function and be played correctly

Producer - A producer is in charge of seeing how the development of the game is going and how it progresses to be nearly finished

VFX Supervisor - A visual effects supervisor’s job is to meet the director and producers creative thoughts in visual effects

Compositor - A compositor’s job is to construct the final image/clip by using various layers of materials

Matchmover - A matchmover’s job is to match the CGI elements and make then into a realistic form that makes it seem live action

Lighting Artist - A lighting artist’s job is to apply all lighting effects to the story and can also add some fake lighting if needed

Rotoscope - A rotoscope artist’s job is to trace over movement made by an actor in a scene and adjust all animation in it frame by frame
